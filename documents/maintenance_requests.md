# Maintenance Requests

*Source: ManageCasa — May 12, 2026*

## Landlord Duty to Repair

Landlords are responsible for keeping the property's essential systems in good working order: plumbing, heating, electrical, structural elements, roofing, windows, and any common areas. This duty is ongoing, not a one-time obligation at the start of the tenancy.

The distinction between landlord repairs and tenant repairs matters significantly. Landlords handle repairs to systems and structural elements. Tenants handle minor maintenance caused by their own use: replacing light bulbs in standard fixtures, minor cleaning, and reporting issues promptly so they do not escalate.

Non-emergency repairs generally require a reasonable time to complete, often interpreted as seven to fourteen days. Landlords who fail to make necessary repairs within a reasonable period expose themselves to rent withholding, repair-and-deduct remedies, and potential damages claims.

## Tenant Responsibility to Report Issues

Tenants are legally prohibited from causing damage to the rental property beyond what results from normal, reasonable use. Tenants cannot allow neglect to cause damage — such as a small water leak that becomes a major mold problem because it was not reported — and cannot conduct activities that accelerate deterioration of the property.

If a tenant fails to report a small plumbing leak and the resulting water damage spreads over months, the tenant may bear partial or full liability for the resulting repairs.

## Tenant Responsibility for Basic Upkeep

Tenants are responsible for keeping the unit reasonably clean and for preventing damage caused by neglect or misuse. This includes routine tasks: disposing of trash promptly, cleaning kitchen appliances, preventing pest conditions caused by unsanitary practices, and maintaining the unit in a condition that does not cause damage to the structure or fixtures.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are landlords responsible for repairs?

Yes. Landlords are generally responsible for maintaining essential systems, structural repairs, and habitability standards. Tenants are usually responsible for damages caused by negligence or misuse.

### Are landlords responsible for appliances?

If appliances are included in the rental, landlords are typically responsible for repairs caused by normal wear. Tenant-caused appliance damage may become the tenant's responsibility.

### Are landlords responsible for light bulbs?

Standard light bulb replacement is usually the tenant's responsibility. Landlords may handle specialty bulbs, exterior lighting, or fixtures related to property safety systems.

### Are landlords responsible for garden and lawn maintenance?

Responsibility depends on the lease. Tenants often handle lawn care for single-family homes, while landlords usually maintain shared exterior areas in multifamily properties.
